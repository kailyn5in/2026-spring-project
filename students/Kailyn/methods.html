Methods

Study Design

In the beginning, we start to watch Twelve Nights, to learn about the shelter, and take some notes.
And we went to the shelter where there are professional people in there.we started getting information from professional people, which we need for helpless dogs.
When we went back to the school, we knew the adoption rates were too low, so we made “fish bones” to find the thing we wanted to work on. Then, we use 5 why to find the action point.
That is “People who don’t care about advertising of shelter.”.
So, we wanted to improve the advertisement, we put all the questions together and discussed it with teachers, after these we fixed all the questions, and started a survey.

1) Initial observation of situation/subject
 - Watch the documentary of Twelve Nights

2) Getting information so that you can design the survey accurately
 1. Getting information from professional people
 2. Going to the shelter

3) Find research topic
 1. Fish bone
 2. 5 whys
 3. Find action point

4) Come up with research question
 1. Do online research
 2. Putting all the questions together
 3. Discussing questions with instructor
 4. Revise the question with instructor advice

Procedure

Our groups are Will, Charlize and me.
We make a plan, that is, Will will be Speaker, catch people and tell people the questions, next is Charlize, she is Recorder, write down what the people said, And about my job, is helper, I’m helper speaker and record at the same time, and also I holded the poster which is about the question list.

1) Form groups

2) Assign jobs for survey
 - Speaker: Catch people and tell them what we are doing. Tell them the question
 - Recorder: Write down the answer
 - Helper: Hold the things to show the options of the questions

3) Go to place for survey

4) Conducting the survey
 1. Introduce ourselves and our project
 2. Collecting data with face-to-face means
 3. Ask the question before asking their age

Special Considerations

In the survey, the speaker needs to speak loudly, and ask one person at the same time.
Also, our group needs to follow these rules, we shouldn’t change the job in our survey.
Then we need to be respectful, we can’t say bad word, sit down and chase the people, that may make people don’t want to talk with us.

- Giving the survey
 - Speak loudly
 - don’t ask more than one person at the same time
 - consistent
  - don’t change the introduction
  - don’t switch job
 - be respectful
  - don’t say bad word
  - don’t sit down
  - don’t chase the people
 - avoid bias

Data Analysis

Finishing the survey, we started compiling the answers, sorting the data and visualizing data.
So we know about these things, 35 year and older people usually like real stories, and we know the conclusion first is most people think animal shelter ads should focus on stories, emotions, and animal pictures.Second,many people do not remember seeing shelter ads, so the ads are not very memorable.
Third,the most memorable parts are real stories, sad or touching scenes, music, and strong visuals.Fourth, to increase pet adoption, ads should make people feel emotionally connected to the animals.
